AI as a force multiplier, not a substitute for design

Teams adopt AI coding tools for speed. Fair. The trap is treating throughput of diffs as progress on architecture. Generated code still has to sit inside a capability map, an ownership model, and a change path the organization can absorb.

A force multiplier amplifies a direction you already chose. If the direction is unclear—shared services vs product silos, buy vs build, synchronous vs evented—AI helps you dig the wrong hole faster.

What AI actually multiplies

Boilerplate, tests scaffolds, migrations of known patterns, and exploration of unfamiliar APIs. It is excellent at local completion when the surrounding design is already coherent.

It is weak at deciding which boundary should exist, which data must not leave a trust zone, or which sequence of releases keeps a regulated estate alive during cutover.

Keep design decisions human

Record the decisions AI cannot own: capability ownership, non-functionals that bind the board, and the explicit “we will not do X.” Review generated change against those records the same way you review human change—only faster on the mechanical parts.

Speed without design ownership is just technical debt with better autocomplete.
