Human-in-the-loop is an architecture decision

Human-in-the-loop sounds safe until the queue is endless, the UI hides context, or overnight batches auto-approve because nobody staffed the rota. The loop is a system component with capacity and failure modes.

Design the work, not the slogan

Who reviews? With what evidence? How long may a case wait? What ships if the reviewer is unavailable? Which actions are never eligible for auto-complete?

Encode those answers in workflow and access control. Training slides do not stop a misconfigured default.

Measure the loop

Track override rates, time-in-queue, and disagreement clusters. Rising overrides mean the model or the process is wrong—not that humans should click faster.
