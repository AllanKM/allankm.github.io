About

Allan Medeiros is a Senior Solutions Architect with more than twenty years building and modernizing enterprise software. He spent fifteen years at IBM — the last five as IT Architect — designing Java systems, integration solutions, and SaaS products for global clients, including CI/CD adoption with Jenkins and Docker and architecture review standards across delivery teams.

At Allianz Technology he works on a cloud insurance platform used by group companies: microservices architecture (Spring Boot, Kubernetes), migration of legacy workloads to AWS, and measurable gains of about 25% lower operating cost and 40% less downtime on the migrated paths. He also defines the specifications, boundaries, and review practices that allow AI coding agents to contribute to those codebases without bypassing security or maintainability requirements.

Education: MEng in Computer Software Engineering (FEUP) — thesis on generative AI agents for ambiguity detection in software requirements — and a BE in Computer Engineering. Peer-reviewed publications are listed on Google Scholar (four papers, ~46 citations); he is co-author of the most-cited entry (IEEE ISC2 2017).

Selected technical focus

  • Cloud insurance platform — service design and delivery on Kubernetes; cross-country requirements; mentoring of engineers on a multi-country team.
  • Legacy-to-cloud migration — AWS target estate; cut downtime ~40% and shortened release cycles on critical insurance workloads.
  • Enterprise integration — APIs and middleware between logistics, support, billing, and vendor systems (IBM account work).
  • AI-assisted delivery controls — specs, guardrails, and human review so agent-generated code can enter regulated production systems safely.
  • Stack — Java, Spring Boot, Kubernetes, Azure/AKS, GCP, AWS, Jenkins, Docker; domains banking, insurance, logistics.
